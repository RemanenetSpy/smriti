Your DNA is NOT
read-only.
DNA is the ultimate
read-only memory.
The hardware of life.
But the hardware can be modified
without changing the code.
Enter DNA Methylation.
Trauma. Diet. Environment.
Experience writes chemical tags onto the DNA, turning genes on or off.
The Hardware Analogy
It’s biological Phase-Change Memory (PCM).
Applying a voltage locks a state.
Living an experience locks a methyl group.
Unlike a genetic mutation,
it’s reversible.
Stable enough to endure.
Malleable enough to adapt.
When the cell divides,
the methyl marks are copied.
The cell remembers what it learned.
Mice taught to fear a scent pass that fear
to their grandchildren.
Memory inherited through chemistry,
not genetics.
The Neural Link
Synapses store short-term patterns.
Epigenetics stores multi-generational states.
If experience writes to biological memory...
What happens when we learn the syntax?
Can we program biology
like hardware?
